  <summary><b>SIM #01 — Escape the Production Incident</b> &nbsp;·&nbsp; choose your path</summary>

  <br/>

  **It's 03:47 AM.** PagerDuty fires. Three pods in `prod-eo-pipeline` are in `CrashLoopBackOff`. The on-call channel is quiet. You have 60 seconds of caffeine left. **What do you do first?**

  <details>
    <summary><b>A)</b> <code>kubectl rollout undo deploy/eo-pipeline</code></summary>

  &nbsp;&nbsp;You roll back. Pods stabilise. But you just reverted a 400-line change without knowing what broke. The PR author sends three question marks at 09:00.<br/>
  <b>Score:</b> saved the page, spent the trust. <b>Lives remaining: 2.</b>

  <details>
    <summary>➜ next: blameless post-mortem</summary>
    You run <code>kubectl describe pod</code> on yesterday's image, find a memory leak in a new Prometheus scrape, raise a PR, write a runbook. Game cleared — but the harder way.
  </details>
  </details>

  <details>
    <summary><b>B)</b> <code>kubectl top pods -n prod-eo-pipeline --sort-by=memory</code></summary>

  &nbsp;&nbsp;You see memory climbing to the limit within 40 seconds. Classic OOM pattern.<br/>
  <b>Next move?</b>

  <details>
    <summary>➜ bump memory limit to buy time</summary>
    Pods stabilise. You now have a morning to find the real leak without a P1 breathing down your neck.<br/>
    <b>Verdict:</b> pragmatic. <b>Lives remaining: 3.</b>
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>➜ dig into a heap dump immediately</summary>
    Noble — but at 03:47 AM, mean-time-to-recovery beats mean-time-to-root-cause. Users don't pay you for heroism, they pay you for uptime.<br/>
    <b>Verdict:</b> correct eventually, wrong first. <b>Lives remaining: 2.</b>
  </details>
  </details>

  <details>
    <summary><b>C)</b> <code>kubectl delete pod --all</code> and hope</summary>

  &nbsp;&nbsp;The chaos option. New pods spin up, OOM again 90 seconds later. PagerDuty refires. Your phone rings.<br/>
  <b>Verdict:</b> this is a speedrun to incident review. <b>Lives remaining: 0.</b> <br/>
  <b>Retry?</b> Scroll back up.
  </details>

</details>
